Mi Quang

Without a doubt, the most underrated dish in Vietnam is Mi Quang. Mi Quang may be available at most restaurants in Vietnam, but it actually originates from Da Nang. Easily distinguished by its yellow-coloured rice noodles, this dish is a hearty mix of bone broth seasoned with fish sauce, black pepper, shallot, and garlic, as well as meaty ingredients such as river shrimp, boiled quail eggs, and roast pork. There is no correct way to cook Mi Quang‚Äîit really depends on your tastes and preferences. To most Danangians, the best Mi Quang is home-made by their mothers or grandmothers, attached with love and memories.

Spring Roll

Another famous Vietnamese food is spring rolls. It's usually served as an appetizer at restaurants in Vietnam. This healthy food is a smart choice when you've eaten too much fried food. Spring rolls have meat, rice noodles, shrimp or seafood, and vegetables, gently rolled in rice paper. You eat it with fish sauce. It costs 8,000 VND per roll. Fried spring rolls were brought to Hanoi from the South and rapidly became a favorite among Hanoians. In honor of its origins, people here call it "Nem Sai Gon".
